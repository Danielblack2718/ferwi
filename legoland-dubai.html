At this popular theme park, you get to build the car you’ve always dreamed of using legos. Explore the theme park and take a look at monuments built using over 20 million lego pieces under one roof. Experience all this and more at LEGOLAND Dubai, a lego themed amusement park for children and adults in the Emirates capital. Zones Inside LEGOLAND® Dubai

The amusement park is divided into six zones, each of which is as good as the next. Make sure to hit them all, and don’t miss out.

Factory

Understand everything that goes into the manufacturing process of the toys that made our childhoods. Get a first-hand glimpse of the anatomy of a LEGO brick when you take a tour of the Factory. As an added bonus, every child gets a newly made brick to take home as a souvenir. 

Miniland

Miniland is an air-conditioned dome structure that hosts the entire Dubai skyline under it, all made of legos. Not only that, take a look at Egypt’s and India’s monuments as well, or build your own city using legos if none of the existing ones are exciting enough! Walk into Miniland, and even the smallest person would feel magnified.

LEGO® CITY

This is a land where dreams have no boundaries. Children can be whoever they want, from being the captain of a ship to being the policeman chasing the bad guys in their dream city that is made from Legos. Here, they can get road training, drive mini-lego cars and get themselves a driving license valid in LEGOLAND.

Kingdoms

Here you can accompany your children on kingdom-themed park rides, from the Dragon roller coaster to Merlin’s Challenge. In the Kingdom zone of LEGOLAND Dubai, kids can have fun and enjoy a magical experience with Merlin, castles, and King’s Market is the main attraction.

Imagination

This is an area where your child gets to play make-believe games. Kids here can play engineer and design their own vehicle, play tourist, and jump on the LEGO train ride. It is a safe space for their mind to run free.

Adventure

Accompany your kids on rides of their lifetime. Watch them battle and save themselves from horrid skeletons on a quest to find the treasure, or explore the ancient Egyptian kingdoms armed with foam fire balls. They can even experience no gravity by being thrown 15 feet in the air on the Beetle Bounce.

Things To Do LEGOLAND® Dubai

In a land where boredom can’t reach you, find a list of exciting activities to do with your children at LEGOLAND Dubai. 

Rides & Attractions

If you are looking for a fun theme park with exciting rides, LEGOLAND has got you covered. Enjoy the Lego-themed rides with your children ranging from dragon-themed ones to high-speed roller coasters. Take a Factory Tour and watch the behind-the-scenes of our beloved toys.

Themed Areas

LEGOLAND has six lego themed zones, with each being entirely different from the previous one. Take a walk through the Dubai skyline made of Legos, explore the monarchy and kingdoms, and watch your children behind the wheel or at the helm of a ship; the experiences are limitless.

Shop

Extend the lovely lego experience by shopping at the Lego store and having a souvenir for the day and the memories.  With numerous outlets throughout the park, you can stop by the King’s Market to get monarchy-themed legos or the Big Shop to visit the largest lego store in the Mideast.

Eat

Fuel up between the day to keep going, and take your pick from a wide range of eateries. You can choose to dine in or take it away. You can also choose between cafe-style options as you have unlimited pizza options, and pasta buffets, all the way to ice cream stores for dessert.

Events

Make festive seasons even more exciting by participating in events hosted by LEGOLAND. Come by for the Bricktacular festival during Christmas or Spooktacular during Halloween and make the celebrations even more fun.

Visitor Tips & Guidelines

  • Comfortable shoes: The park spans 14 hectares, so make sure you don't wear comfortable walking shoes while exploring. 
  • Plan your visit: While winter months are everyone's first choice to visit. To avoid crowds, try visiting in summer after sunset hours. 
  • Book online: During holidays or the festive season, only a few ticket booths at the venue are open, leading to long queues. Make sure to book your tickets online. 
  • Waiting time: Most roller coasters and rides leave every other seat vacant to adhere to social distancing norms, leading to longer wait times. Visit at a time when you anticipate lesser crowds. 
  • Height restrictions: Before purchasing your ticket, make sure your child meets the height criteria for the rides [mentioned online], as the park is strict regarding safety. 
  • Carry water: While food is not allowed, carry an abundance of water. Outdoor rides are shaded, but the weather and walking would make you thirsty.
