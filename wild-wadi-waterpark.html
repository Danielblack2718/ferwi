Why Visit Wild Wadi Water Park?

Located against the beautiful backdrop of the Burj Al Arab, the Wild Wadi Water Park is home to a variety of exciting rides and slides. From breathtaking roller coasters to a child-friendly play area, the Wild Wadi has something for everyone. And it is for that very reason that it has become one of the most popular destinations in Dubai. Be it Jumeirah Sceirah, one of the tallest free-fall water rides in the world, or the Master Blaster roller coaster that propels you almost 15 meters above the ground, you will thoroughly enjoy your visit to the oldest water park in Dubai. Let’s take a look at Wild Wadi tickets, opening hours, and everything else you need to know for a fun-filled vacation.

Wild Wadi Rides

With over 30 rides to its name, Wild Wadi is a one-stop-destination for everyone visiting Dubai. Perhaps the most famous among all the Wild Wadi rides is the Jumeirah Sceirah - the fastest free-fall water slide in the Middle East. Equally exciting is the Burj Surj that takes you on a roller-coaster ride of turns and slides, ultimately leading you to a large pool. If you are an adrenaline junkie, the Action River with its whirlpools and river rapids is a must-visit.

Jumeirah Sceirah

The Jumeirah Sceirah water slide is a sight to behold, standing tall at 33 meters and located in the scenic Wild Wadi Waterpark in Dubai. This thrilling ride will give you an adrenaline-filled experience as soon as you launch yourself down the steep descent of the water slide.

Burj Surj

Burj Surj is one of the tallest and most thrilling attractions in Wild Wadi Waterpark. This massive tower stands at a towering height of 33 meters (108 feet). The ride begins with a steep ascent up the tower, where guests soar above the ground on an adrenaline-filled journey.

Action River

Action River in Wild Wadi Waterpark is a thrilling ride for water-lovers. The action starts just by getting in the river: you'll be sent down a steep decline, with twists and turns that make your heart race as you spin around.

Juha's Journey

Enjoy the thrills and spills of Juha’s Journey, a winding rapids ride through the park that takes you on an adventure through dark tunnels and around tight turns. Feel the rush as you make your way around high-speed rapids and dip through deep dips into swirling pools of water below.

Breaker's Bay

Breaker's Bay at Wild Wadi Waterpark is an adventure paradise for thrill seekers and fun seekers alike. The waves range from gentle swells to hefty breakers, providing a unique experience each time you enter the water. With a capacity of over 2,000 swimmers, this expansive beach-style pool offers something for everyone.

Tantrum Alley

Tantrum Alley at Wild Wadi Waterpark is an adrenaline-filled adventure that is sure to get your heart racing! Tantrum Alley is an aquatic river rapids ride that will throw you into a whirlwind of swirling waves and breathtaking drops. It features two huge corkscrews, steep drops and exhilarating 360 degree turns.

What are Wild Wadi timings?

Wild Wadi opening times are as follows:

Monday to Sunday: 10.00 AM to 6:00 PM.

What is the best time to visit Wild Wadi?

Since Wild Wadi water park is one of the most popular destinations in the city, it sees a large number of visitors every day. One way to avoid the crowd is to arrive at the park as soon as it opens at 10.00 AM. Evenings tend to be the busiest so it is best to avoid arriving post 4.00 PM. 

The summer months between June to September are also the busiest during the year due to the holiday season. If you're looking to avoid the crowd, October to April is best.

How long should I spend at Wild Wadi?

You can spend anywhere from 1 to 4 hours, as the waterpark is quite large and has a wide variety of rides and attractions within that you won't want to miss out on.

Where is Wild Wadi located?

Wild Wadi is located in Dubai’s Jumeirah, against the backdrop of the iconic Burj Al Arab.

How can I reach Wild Wadi?

Wild Wadi is accessible by bus, metro, and taxi.

Bus: Take Bus 8 or 81 and get off at the Wild Wadi bus stop, which is a 3 minute walk away from the waterpark.

Metro: Take the M1 and get off at the Mall of Emirates station, post which you can take a 6 minute cab ride to Wild Wadi waterpark.

Taxi: Taking a taxi from the airport only takes around a half hour.

How far is Wild Wadi from the airport?

The Wild Wadi water park is located at a distance of 25 kilometers from the airport and takes about half an hour by car.

What are the rules I have to follow while visiting Wild Wadi?

Here's a list of Wild Wadi rules you can refer to while planning your visit.

What can I eat at Wild Wadi?

Since outside food is not allowed, you cannot carry your own food inside the water park. However, Wild Wadi houses a number of cafes where you can dine in.

Does Wild Wadi rides have height restrictions?

Visitors must be at least 1.1 metres to be eligible to enjoy certain Wild Wadi rides. Here's a complete list of Wild Wadi Waterpark rules and regulations.

What should I wear to Wild Wadi Waterpark?

While there is no strict dress code, it is important that you carry a swimsuit to enjoy the waterpark rides to their fullest as you will only be allowed on the rides with appropriate swimwear on. It is important to note that wearing jewellery inside the waterpark is prohibited.

Who can go on the rides at Wild Wadi Waterpark?

The rides at Wild Wadi Waterpark are open to everyone except individuals who are pregnant or have a medical condition or physical restrictions, as most of the rides require a lot of activity and movement.

How old do you have to be for the rides at Wild Wadi Waterpark?

Individuals under the age of 13 have to be accompanied by someone who is 16 years or older. Younger children and toddlers are welcome to go on safe rides provided they wear special swim diapers if needed. Children under the age of 3 get complimentary access to the waterpark.
